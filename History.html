History

History of Bacolod City

Bacolod, often referred to as the "City of Smiles," traces its history back to the early 18th century when settlers from Panay Island established a small settlement along the banks of the Magsungay River.

The city experienced significant growth during the Spanish colonial period, becoming a hub for sugarcane production and trade. It was officially recognized as a town in 1755 and later as a city in 1938.

Bacolod played a crucial role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule, and later, during the American period, it emerged as a center of commerce and education in the Visayas region.

Today, Bacolod is known for its rich cultural heritage, vibrant festivals, and warm hospitality, making it a popular destination for tourists and a beloved home for its residents.

Famous Festival: MassKara Festival

The MassKara Festival is Bacolod City's most famous and colorful celebration, attracting thousands of visitors from around the world each year. The festival, which began in the 1980s as a response to a period of economic downturn, is characterized by vibrant street dancing, dazzling masks adorned with smiling faces, and lively music. It is a symbol of Bacolod's resilience, creativity, and spirit of community, earning the city its nickname, the "City of Smiles." The MassKara Festival is a time of joyous revelry, where locals and tourists alike come together to celebrate Bacolod's rich culture and heritage.
